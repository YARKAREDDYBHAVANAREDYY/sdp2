TYPES OF SOILS

Alluvial soils

Alluvial soils are soils deposited by surface water. They yield splendid crops of rice, wheat, sugarcane, tobacco, cotton, jute, maize.

Black soils

Black soils include wheat, jowar,sunflower and millets. Rice and sugarcane are equally important where irrigation facilities are available.

Red and Yellow soils

Red soil develops on crystalline igneous rocks.The crop that grows are rice, wheat, sugarcane, millet, groundnut, ragi and potato.

Laterite soils

Laterite soils lack fertility due to intensive leaching.laterites are suitable for growing plantation crops like tea, coffee, rubber, cinchona, coconut.

Arid soils

Arid soils range from red to brown in colour.crops such as barley, cotton, wheat, millets, maize, pulses grow here.

Saline soils

They are also known as Usara soils. Saline soils contain a larger proportion.Barley,cotton are grown

Peaty soils

They are found in the areas of heavy rainfall.Crops such as potatoes, sugar beet, celery, onions, carrots grown.

Forest soils

The soils vary in structure and texture depending on the mountain environment where they are formed. Coconut, barley, spices, tea, cashew, maize, coffee, palm grown.

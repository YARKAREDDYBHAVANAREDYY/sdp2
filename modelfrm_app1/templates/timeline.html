Timeline of Growing Fish

BroodStock

+2 Years

Broodstock are adult fish used for breeding. ... Injured fish can get an infection, and may die. Fish with eggs will not spawn if handled roughly. Hatcheries without broodstock have to collect adult fish from nearby ponds, tanks or small reservoirs.

Hatchery

Fish Hatchery

A fish hatchery is a place for artificial breeding, egg hatching, and rearing across the early life stages of aquatic animals (e.g. finfish and crustaceans). ... These young small fish are then transferred to an on-growing section to reach harvest size.

spawning

Spawn

Spawn is the eggs and sperm released or deposited into water by aquatic animals. ... The process of spawning typically involves females releasing ova (unfertilized eggs) into the water, often in large quantities, while males simultaneously or sequentially release spermatozoa (milt) to fertilize the eggs.

Nursery

15 - 20 Days(Fry)/h3>

An incomplete-system pond farm where young fish are raised for subsequent transfer to fish farms, where market weight is attained.

Fingerlings

60 - 90 Days

When they have developed to the point where they are capable of feeding themselves, the fish are called fry. When, in addition, they have developed scales and working fins, the transition to a juvenile fish is complete and it is called a fingerling. Fingerlings are typically about the size of fingers.

Growing Out Systems

10 - 12 Months

There are four main aquaculture grow out systems/techniques (or combinations of these). These four aquaculture grow out techniques are pond, cage, flow through or RAS. Based upon the climate, availability of water, land and electricity the decision of aquaculture grow out systems are made.
